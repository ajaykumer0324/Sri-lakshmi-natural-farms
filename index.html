Our Mushrooms & Uses

Milky Mushroom

Also called Calocybe indica, milky mushrooms are prized for their firm texture and long shelf life in warm climates.

Uses & Benefits

  • Culinary: Great in curries, stir-fries, gravies and dry snacks. Holds shape well during cooking.
  • Nutrition: Good source of protein, dietary fiber, B-vitamins and minerals like potassium and selenium.
  • Health: Supports immunity, provides antioxidants; low in fat and calories ‚Äî ideal for healthy diets.
  • Storage & Handling: Keep refrigerated in paper bags; use within 3‚Äì5 days for best taste.
  • Market value: Popular with local markets and restaurants due to shelf life and texture.

Oyster Mushroom

Delicate flavour and soft texture; cooks quickly and blends well into many dishes.

Uses & Benefits

  • Culinary: Excellent in soups, noodles, pakoras, and as a meat substitute in vegetarian dishes.
  • Nutrition: Rich in protein, fiber, vitamins (especially B-complex) and minerals.
  • Health: Contains compounds that may support heart health and cholesterol management.
  • Storage & Handling: Refrigerate and consume within 2‚Äì4 days; keep dry and avoid vacuum sealing wet mushrooms.
  • Market value: Consistently popular for restaurants and home cooks due to flavour and versatility.

Pink Oyster Mushroom

Vibrant colour and unique texture ‚Äî eye-catching and nutritious.

Uses & Benefits

  • Culinary: Adds colour and texture to salads, grills, and fusion dishes. Briefly saut√© to preserve colour.
  • Nutrition: High in protein, antioxidants and micronutrients.
  • Health: Antioxidant-rich; may support overall wellbeing and immune function.
  • Storage & Handling: More delicate ‚Äî use quickly within 1‚Äì3 days for best colour and texture.
  • Market value: Great for premium packs and restaurants that want visual impact on plates.

How these mushrooms help people

Mushrooms are a sustainable, nutritious food source. They provide plant-based protein and important micronutrients while using relatively little land and water compared to animal protein. Introducing these varieties to local diets can:

  • Improve access to affordable, nutrient-dense food.
  • Offer new flavours and textures, helping households diversify meals.
  • Create local economic opportunities ‚Äî selling to restaurants, markets and direct customers.
  • Support healthier eating patterns (low fat, low calorie, high fiber).
